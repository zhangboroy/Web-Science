(Need a nonstick skillet? Here's our guide to how to buy the best.)1/7Photo by Chelsea Kyle, Food Styling by Anna StockwellPour & ScramblePour the whisked eggs into the hot skillet and immediately start scrambling them with a rubber spatula.2/7Photo by Chelsea Kyle, Food Styling by Anna StockwellShake & FillDon't scramble too long, though. As soon as the eggs start to set, stop scrambling and shake the skillet a bit so the eggs form an even, solid layer. Quickly sprinkle whatever fillings you desire down the middle of the eggs (here we're using goat cheese and herbs).3/7Photo by Chelsea Kyle, Food Styling by Anna StockwellTilt & RollNow tilt the pan over the burner, and start to gently roll the eggs away from you with your rubber spatula. Don't worry if the eggs are still a little runny at this stage—they'll continue to set as you roll.4/7Photo by Chelsea Kyle, Food Styling by Anna StockwellKeep Rolling & RollingContinue your slow, gentle rolling until you reach the edge of the eggs.5/7Photo by Chelsea Kyle, Food Styling by Anna StockwellFlip & FinishComplete your egg roll by tipping the egg out of the pan and on to a plate.6/7Photo by Chelsea Kyle, Food Styling by Anna StockwellYou made an omelette!Voila!
