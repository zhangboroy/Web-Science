Theo Walcott believes Arsenal captain Per Mertesacker will make an unbelievable manager

Metro Sport Reporter for Metro.co.ukWednesday 25 Jan 2017 9:38 pm 










Per Mertesacker signed a new one-year deal with Arsenal earlier this month (Photo credit should read IKIMAGES/AFP/Getty Images)
Theo Walcott believes Arsenal captain Per Mertesacker possesses all the qualities required to become an ‘unbelievable’ manager in the future.
FA Cup quarter-final draw: Chelsea host Man Utd as Lincoln discover incredible tie
The former Germany international has missed the entire season to date having suffered a serious knee injury in pre-season, but is held in such high esteem by Arsene Wenger that he moved to extend the 32-year-old’s contract by another year earlier this month.
Walcott and Mertesacker often celebrate together after Arsenal victories. (Picture: Catherine Ivill – AMA/Getty Images)
Walcott, who revealed he enjoys a close bond with the World Cup winner, is confident that Mertesacker will enjoy a long and successful career in management once he hangs up his boots.
He told Arsenal.com: ‘Per Mertesacker, where do I start with him. He’s very serious and he’s a leader on the pitch and off it.
‘You always want to listen to him and I think he’ll be an unbelievable manager. Geneuinely, out of all the players I’ve worked with he’d be the best manager.’
