Arsenal and Chelsea offered Karim Benzema as Real Madrid look to offload striker

gossipSimon Rice for Metro.co.ukSunday 29 Jan 2017 11:24 pm 










Karim Benzema in action on Sunday (Getty)
Real Madrid striker Karim Benzema has been offered to both Arsenal and Chelsea, according to reports.
Spanish outlet Diario Gol claim that Real Madrid president Florentino Perez is ready to offload the France international.
FA Cup quarter-final draw: Chelsea host Man Utd as Lincoln discover incredible tie
He has reportedly grown frustrated with the 29-year-old, who has not been as prolific this season as in previous campaigns. However he does still have 12 goals to his name.
Real Madrid’s recent surprise exit from the Copa del Rey was apparently the last straw for Perez, while fans booed the striker at the Bernabeu on Sunday during their win over Real Sociedad.
Perez has reportedly told Arsenal and Chelsea of his intentions to sell Benzema, although Paris St Germain will also pursue the striker.

Benezema has long been linked with a move to the Emirates with his style and nationality making him an obvious fit. Meanwhile Chelsea manager Antonio Conte is thought to be in the market for an additional striker to complement and back up Diego Costa.
