Arsene Wenger says Danny Welbeck has done enough to keep his Arsenal place against Watford

Coral Barry for Metro.co.ukMonday 30 Jan 2017 9:52 am 










Danny Welbeck returned to the starting eleven in lethal form (Picture: Getty)
Arsene Wenger has insisted Arsenal need to be cautious with Danny Welbeck despite admitting the striker has done enough to keep his place in the side.
FA Cup quarter-final draw: Chelsea host Man Utd as Lincoln discover incredible tie
Arsenal welcome Watford to the Emirates on Tuesday and Welbeck will be hopeful his keeps his starting spot after putting two past Southampton at the weekend.
Welbeck was making his first start since May 2016, but Wenger said Arsenal cannot rush the striker back.
‘Of course he’s done enough to keep his place at the moment have to be cautious,’ said Wenger in his press conference.

‘Every three days is a bit short.’
Arsenal have made do without a second senior striker for much of the season with Olivier Giroud the only other frontman in the squad in Welbeck’s absence.
Giroud has spent much of his season warming the bench, but calls have been increasing that Welbeck be restored to the Premier League eleven.

But Wenger insisted the England international is still a few weeks away from full fitness.
‘He’s very sharp but we are still not out of it completely, still need to be cautious, physically he’s ready to play but we still to have manage the times we use him and the times we rest him,’ said Wenger.
‘For a while, the next month, he will be completely fit.
