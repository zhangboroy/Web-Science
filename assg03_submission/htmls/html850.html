Arsene Wenger's act fools the FA after Arsenal boss claims he was unclear where he was meant to stand following sending off against Burnley

  • Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger is banned from the touchline for four matches
  • Wenger was punished for pushing Anthony Taylor during the Burnley match 
  • His punishment suggests the FA are suckers for the Monsieur Professeur act
  • Read: Pele, Muhammad Ali, Usain Bolt and Jack Nicklaus... Serena Williams is as great as any of them

By Martin Samuel for the Daily Mail

Published: 17:52 EST, 29 January 2017 | Updated: 06:52 EST, 30 January 2017

Arsene Wenger appeared in person before the Football Association commission, and the outcome suggests they are suckers for that Monsieur Professeur act, too. 

Wenger said he did not go down the tunnel after his dismissal against Burnley, because he was unclear where he was supposed to stand after being sent off. Really? And they fell for it?

‘In 2009, I had to go in the stand at Old Trafford and I didn’t know where to go,’ he explained. ‘No one tells you what you have to do when you are sent off.’ Yes, and there’s a reason for that. You’re not supposed to get sent off. There’s no ‘when’ about it.

Arsene Wenger got a four-game ban for his altercation with Anthony Taylor in the Burnley win

For the same reason, the average bloke won’t know the immediate procedure after being sent to prison. And, maybe, at Old Trafford, the procedure and geography might be a little confusing. The tunnel is in the corner, the dug out on the halfway line. A visiting manager might be unsure. Surely they don’t expect him to walk 50 yards past rows and rows of Manchester United fans?

But Wenger wasn’t away from home this time. He was at the Emirates Stadium, an arena he helped design. The tunnel is adjacent to the dug-out, the instruction from the referee clear. Vacate your pitch-side seat. It really doesn’t matter where you go. Just go. 

The Arsenal boss watched his side beat Southampton 5-0 from the stands on Saturday

Equally, there is no absence of clarity around pushing the fourth official. Even if Wenger was entirely perplexed over his exit route, he would know that to make physical contact with Anthony Taylor was wrong.

Still, no doubt Wenger gave his explanation calmly, eloquently, and with a hint of the half-smile that suggests he is the smartest guy in the room, and the commission fell for it, as so many do.

Four-game ban, the first match taking place in a competition Wenger rates so highly he starts Rob Holding at the back. He got away with it there. Smart work, Professeur. 

Wenger was sent to the stands at Old Trafford late on during Arsenal's 2-1 loss to Manchester United in 2009
