Arsenal kit power rankings: all 47 of their Premier League jerseys ranked

The Gunners have had a total of 47 kits during the Premier League era, but not all jerseys are created equal

  •  Share
  •  Comments
ByHamish Mackay
  • 11:26, 22 MAR 2016
  • Updated09:08, 4 JUL 2016

From the angular, psychedelic glory of the 'bruised banana' shirt to the clean-cut simplicity of the 125th year anniversary jersey, Arsenal have produced some of the Premier League's greatest kits.

But not all kits are created equal, and some of the Gunners' strips are better left in the memory's periphery.

In total, Arsenal have had 17 home and 31 away kits during the Premier League era... but where do they rank against each other?

In a totally scientific and not at all subjective approach, we've managed to order every kit from ugliest through to the best.

47. Third kit 2014-15

There is so much wrong with this kit. From the button down collar to the diagonal stripes and lime green trim, it's a monstrosity.

46. Third 2010-11

Maroon shorts and a mustard yellow shirt? No thanks.

45. Away 2011-12

This racing silk style away kit would look more at place at Aintree than Arsenal.

44. Away 2014-15

Whose idea was it to have a deep v-neck that exposes the top of their chests? Awful.

43. Away 2012-13

Purple, black and pink? No.

42. Away 1993-94

Uninspiring at the time and hasn't aged particularly well.

41. Away 1997-99

More than a few good games in this kit, but it's nothing special.

40. Away 2015-16

Gold always looks good on Arsenal, shame about the diamond pattern on the torso.

39. Third 2015-16

Not a typical Arsenal colour scheme, but Puma get away with it because it looks good.

38. Home 2008-10

Looked great when it came out, hasn't aged well.

37. Away 2007-08 (and third kit for 2008-09)

Nice continuation of claret from the previous home kit, went well with the white.

36. Third 2007-08

The collar is a bit big but again the claret looks good, especially with the gold trim. Barely warn in competitive games.

35. Third 2009-10

A brilliantly simple white shirt that worked well with grey shorts and socks.

34. Home 1992-94

A lovely home shirt that is so popular you cans till buy it from the Arsenal shop.

33. Away 2008-09

A lovely ode to the 88-91 away kit. it also brings back memories of Andrey Arshavin's four-goal haul at Anfield.

32. Away 1996-97

Nice shade of yellow, nice shade of blue. Pretty unremarkable, though.

31. Home 2015-16

A grower. Not initially that popular but it's a pretty solid home shirt. Shame there won't be many good memories in it.

30. Third 1994-96

One of the last of the 'massive badge' era.

29. Home 2006-08

Arsenal's home shirt is defined as red with white sleeves. This is exactly that. Hard to love or dislike in any way.

28. Home 2005-06

The kit for Arsenal's final season at Highbury. It didn't look good on fans, but the players made it work.

27. Away 1999-2001

Classic away kit, not much wrong with it.

26. Third 2000-02

Lovely shade of blue and very simple after some hectic nineties kits.

25. Third 1998-99 (only worn once)

Nice colours, simple, ranked relatively highly for its novelty value - it was only ever used in one match, against Lens.

24. Home 2014-15

A great debut home shirt from Puma. Shame it's so tight. Tight kits always felt like Tottenham's thing.

23. Home 2012-14

Worn when Arsenal finally broke their trophy hodoo in 2014, coming from 2-0 down to beat Hull 3-2 in the FA Cup final.

22. Home 2000-02

A classic home kit from a brilliant era.

20=. Home 1999-2000

That the white ended before the shoulder made it better than 00-02.

20=. Home 1998-1999

See above.

19. Home 1996-98

Arsene Wenger's first Arsenal two trophies (the Premier League and FA Cup double) were won in this kit.

17=. Away 2001-02

A lovely gold kit worn by a squad good enough to pull off the colour.

17=. Third 2002-03

See above.

16. Home 2010-11

The archetypal Arsenal home kit.

15. Away 2009-10

Great kit, not worn by a particularly great squad.

14. Away 2002-03

Great colour scheme, brilliantly faded together.

12=. Away 2006-07

Nice shades of yellow and grey.

12=. Away 2005-06

See above. Plus, Arsenal wore it in their only Champions League final.

11. Away 1994-95

Nice big badge, crew neck and a colour at the same time plus a great lightning bolt pattern underneath.

10. Home 2011-12

Arsenal's 125th anniversary shirt with a well-designed one-off badge. Some great games in this kit too (Arsenal 5-2 Tottenham, Henry scoring on return against Leeds).

9. Away 2013-14

The socks alone send this kit into the top ten.

8. Home 2004-05

A bit busy but a brilliant kit. It was also the last season Arsenal had a truly great squad.

7 . Away 2004-05 (and third in 2005-06)

Every Arsenal player looked good in this.

6. Away 2003-04

The best yellow away kit of the Wenger era, worn by the best players of the Wenger era.

5. Away 1991-93

Probably the best yellow away kit in Arsenal's history.

3=. Home 1994-96

Simple, but still so clearly a classic nineties kit.

3=. Home 1994-96 (with collar)

Just look at those socks.

2. Away 1995-96

Lightning bolts in different shades of blue? Yes please.

1. Home 2002-04

A brilliant, simple home kit worn by arguably the best Arsenal side in history.

*Technically, Arsenal also had a blue third kit for the 2006-07 season (it was the same as 04-05 way kit but with a different sponsor) but they never played in it and so has not been included in this list.
