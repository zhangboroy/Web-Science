How to Make a Classic Omelette

Work low and slow, and the perfect omelette—no browning!—can be yours morning, noon, or night.

Anna Stockwell 12.30.15

Whisk & Melt

Before you begin, throughly whisk three eggs with a bit of salt and pepper, then melt a tablespoon or two of butter in a small nonstick skillet over medium. (Need a nonstick skillet? Here's our guide to how to buy the best.)

1/7

Pour & Scramble

Pour the whisked eggs into the hot skillet and immediately start scrambling them with a rubber spatula.

2/7

Shake & Fill

Don't scramble too long, though. As soon as the eggs start to set, stop scrambling and shake the skillet a bit so the eggs form an even, solid layer. Quickly sprinkle whatever fillings you desire down the middle of the eggs (here we're using goat cheese and herbs).

3/7

Tilt & Roll

Now tilt the pan over the burner, and start to gently roll the eggs away from you with your rubber spatula. Don't worry if the eggs are still a little runny at this stage—they'll continue to set as you roll.

4/7

Keep Rolling & Rolling

Continue your slow, gentle rolling until you reach the edge of the eggs.

5/7

Flip & Finish

Complete your egg roll by tipping the egg out of the pan and on to a plate.

6/7

You made an omelette!

Voila! You have a perfectly rolled omelette without any browning on it—just how an omlette should be.

7/7
